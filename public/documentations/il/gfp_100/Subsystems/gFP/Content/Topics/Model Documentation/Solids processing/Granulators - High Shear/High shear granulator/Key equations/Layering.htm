Layering

Layering is characterised by an increase in particle size as fine powder particles deposit on the surfaces of wet granules.

The moisture content is evaluated as a mass fraction of external liquid in for the system as a whole. The increase in granule size due to layering is divided amongst the fine powder, liquid, and void components based on their respective volume fractions outside the granulate phase. The total increase in the mass of these components in the granulate phase are balanced by a decrease in the masses of the fine powder and liquid phases. It is assumed that all fine powder is small enough that powder particle size does not play a role in layering.

It should be noted that since the linear growth rate due to layering is typically not dependent on granule size, small granules experience a more dramatic increase in size than large granules because of their high surface area-to-volume ratios. As a result, breakage can induce layering by increasing the overall surface area of the granulate phase.
