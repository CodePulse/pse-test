Seed pot

The Seed_pot model allows the seeding of crystals at multiple times throughout a process, and also initially (already possible through the “Initial PSD” tab within the crystallizer models). This model essentially allows more flexibility on the time of seeding, critical to the evolution of seeded crystallization processes. At industrial scale it is common to spread the total seed amount over a number of seeding points, for various reasons, such as the following:

  • Unknown or poor knowledge of the solubility and hence the degree of supersaturation/under-saturation for the full process range of conditions, such as temperature and solvent composition.
  • Increased impurities at pilot/plant scale from the lab scale, affecting the solubility under the same conditions
  • Variabilities of charges of solvents, solutes, etc. at pilot/plant scale, yielding a predicted solubility band for a given batch/process, dependent upon the accuracies of the charging equipment flow meters, pumps, balances, etc.
  • Optimisation strategies to achieve a specified product PSD.
