Number of transfer units

The performance of a convective dryer can be described by specifying the number of transfer units of the unit, this simple relationship gives how far along the operating line the outlet conditions will be. For a deep bed or fluidised bed in early stages of drying this value would be 10, for a spray dryer the number of transfer units would be between 1 and 2 (typically), see Kemp (2011)1 Kemp, I.C., (2011) Drying Models, Myths and Misconceptions. Chemical Engineering and Technology, 34(7), 1057-1066. for further details.

Macro-mass balance

A macro mass balance is performed based on the dryer capacity calculated from the psychrometric calculations. The solid and vapour phase are considered to be in thermal equilibrium when exiting the dryer so both the vapour and solid temperature are assigned the operating temperature of the dryer.
