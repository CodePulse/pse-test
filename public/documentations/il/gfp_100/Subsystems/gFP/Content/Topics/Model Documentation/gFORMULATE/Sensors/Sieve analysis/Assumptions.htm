Assumptions

In performing sieve analysis based on the particle size distribution in the stream, the following assumptions are made.

  • The unit does not affect material being analysed in any way.
  • Perfect separation of particles is assumed to occur in the sieves.
  • All particles are assumed to be hard spheres.
  • If the whole of a discretisation bin falls within a sieve range (defined as the difference between two successive sieve sizes), all particles in that discretisation bin are allocated to that sieve range.
  • If the upper part of a discretisation bin falls within a sieve range, then a fraction of the particles in that bin are allocated to that sieve range. The fraction allocated is the same on a mass basis as the fraction of the bin width on a particle size basis that falls within the sieve range.
  • Similarly, if the lower part of a discretisation bin falls within a sieve range, then a fraction of the particles in that bin are allocated to that sieve range. The fraction allocated is the same on a mass basis as the fraction of the bin width on a particle size basis that falls within the sieve range.
