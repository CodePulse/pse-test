Johnson (1972) model

The Johnson (1972) 1Johnson, M.C.R. (1972) ‘Particle size distribution of the active ingredient for solid dosage forms of low dosage’, Pharma. Acta Helv., 546-559. model for content uniformity is further sub-divided depending on whether the API within the solid dosage form is considered as single sized or to have a Particle size distribution (PSD) and the level of the drug/API dose within the solid preparation. The level of the API/drug dose is determined from the mass of the dose and of the tablet/capsules specifications on the dialog.

Single sized particles

For cases where the API within the solid dosage form is considered as single sized, the content uniformity can be evaluated as follows:

(1)

Notation

  Relative standard deviation %
  Characteristic particle size m
  True mass density of the drug/API kg/m3
  Mass of dose in solid dosage form kg

The CU sensor allows for the specification of the characteristic particle size in a number of ways, as follows:

  • D50 (Median) – evaluated using the entire PSD for the selected particulate phase in the PSD_sensor model,
  • D32 – evaluated using the entire PSD for the selected particulate phase in the PSD_sensor model,
  • D43 – evaluated using the entire PSD for the selected particulate phase in the PSD_sensor model,
  • D63 - evaluated using the entire PSD for the selected particulate phase in the PSD_sensor model,
  • User specified – allows the entry of a representative particle size.

PSD – low dosage preparations – below 1% wt. API/drug content

For low dosage preparations, with API/drug content in the solid dosage form of 1 wt% or less:

(2)

Notation

  Relative standard deviation %
  Particle size m
  Mass fraction with particle size kg/kg
  True mass density of the drug/API kg/m3
  Mass of dose in solid dosage form kg

PSD – low dosage preparations 1 - 10 wt% API/drug

For low dosage preparations, with API/drug content in the solid dosage form of between 1 wt% and 10 %wt.:

(3)

Notation

  Relative standard deviation %
  Particle size m
  Mass fraction with particle size kg/kg
  True mass density of the drug/API kg/m3
  Mass of dose in solid dosage form kg
  Mass fraction of the non-drug components, such as excipients in the mixture/final solid dosage form kg/kg

PSD – above 10% API/drug

For dosage levels above 10%, the RSD of the solid dosage form can be evaluated using the following:

(4)

Notation

  Relative standard deviation %
  Mean particle mass with particle size kg
  Mass fraction with particle size kg/kg
  Total tablet/sample mass kg
  Mass fraction of the drug/API in the mixture/final dosage form kg/kg
  Mass fraction of the non-drug components, such as excipients in the mixture/final solid dosage form kg/kg

It should be noted, that in the current implementation the PSD of the API/drug is also employed as the PSD for the excipient or the other major component in the SODF. In this case, in the above equation the terms and are equivalent.
