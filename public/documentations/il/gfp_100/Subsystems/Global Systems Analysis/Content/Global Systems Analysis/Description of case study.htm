Description of the illustrative example system

A single system is used, in this guide, to illustrate the different types of analysis GSA can be used for, from both system and modelling perspectives.

Consider a simple continuous flow stirred tank reactor (CSTR), as shown in the figure below. The variables in blue are typical factors and the variables in red are typical responses (which may vary for each case).

Schematic of the reactor layout with factors (blue) and responses (red)

Notation

  Ci Molar concentration of component i
  Fin Inlet molar flow rate
  Fout Outlet molar flow rate
  T Temperature
  Tcoolant Coolant temperature
  Xin,i Inlet molar fraction of component i

Methyl acetate and methanol are fed to the reactor, where the reactions below take place, to produce acetic acid and acetic anhydride:

Notation

  AcAc Acetic acid
  AcAn Acetic anhydride
  MeAc Methyl acetate
  MeOH Methanol

For simplicity, the reaction is slightly exothermic and the reactor temperature is controlled by the temperature of the coolant. Since the reactor is an overflow type, the flow rate out of the reactor is determined by the liquid level above the pipe height.
