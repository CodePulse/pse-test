Workarounds for dynamic models

By default, GSA collects results at the end of the time horizon. While for steady-state simulation this is at time equal to zero, for dynamic simulation it is the end of the simulation. Therefore, to gain insight into dynamic behaviour, two workarounds can be used:

  • To obtain results at different times simply define a variable, for example, sim_time, and include a SCHEDULE section containing statement CONTINUE FOR sim_time. Then specify sim_time as a factor to obtain results at different times.

  • Alternatively, to investigate the effect of an operating procedure on a response, add a more elaborate SCHEDULE section which includes multiple factors. For example, to investigate the effect of feed change over time you use the SCHEDULE section below:

    SCHEDULE 
 SEQUENCE

  REASSIGN
    R101.Feed.Flow := Flow_interval_1;
  END

  CONTINUE FOR Interval_1_duration

  REASSIGN
    R101.Feed.Flow := Flow_interval_2;
  END
  
  CONTINUE FOR Interval_2_duration
  ...

    Then specify Flow_interval_i and Interval_i_duration as factors.

However, note the observations below:

  • A dynamic simulation requires an initial condition, typically at steady-state. Be mindful of the conditions under which the system is started and, in case non-steady-state initial conditions are specified, what state these represent.

  • Results are based on the combined influence of all trajectories and it is not possible to distinguish between the different trajectories.
