gCOAS Dosage Forms

The gCOAS Dosage Forms library contains models for orally administered formulations, intravenous injections and infusions. The models are used in conjunction with the in vitro, in vivo and pharmacokinetics models. In gPROMS FormulatedProducts, three new models have been added to the library; the Material sampler, Dosage sampler, and Modified release tablet dose. The material sampler can be used to sample material from a solid manufacturing process and dose it into an in vitro vessel or the gastrointestinal tract. Similarly, the dosage sampler can be used to sample a dosage form (e.g. a tablet from a tablet press) from a solid manufacturing process and dose it into an in vitro vessel or the gastrointestinal tract. The modified release tablet dose enables users to administer dosage forms that are composed of hierarchical solid material (e.g. granules made from primary particles). When dosed, nested material (e.g. primary particles) is first released from the composite material (e.g. granules) before subsequently dissolving. In addition to the new models, a number of enhancements have been made to the legacy dosage form models. In particular, full reaction equilibria has been incorporated into the solution and suspension dose models for rigorous, dynamic handling of liquid composition, pH and solubility. Other highlights include new specification options for characterising the size and shape of solid particles, support for Global System Analysis and additional flexibility when scheduling dosing regimens.
