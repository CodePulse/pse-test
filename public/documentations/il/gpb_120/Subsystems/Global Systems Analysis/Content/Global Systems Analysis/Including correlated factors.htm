Including correlated factors/multivariate distributions

Correlated factors are only of interest when distributions are used, and therefore are of no consequence for scenario or parametric analysis.

At present, correlated factors can only be included for uncertainty analysis. This of interest when information about the correlation, in the form of a covariance matrix, is available (for example, as supplied by a parameter estimation). Note that it is possible to have a covariance matrix with a size proportional to the number of factors, that is, a full covariance matrix, or multiple subgroups instead.

To include a correlation between factors, reproduce the steps below.

  1. Specify all factors of interest
  2. Select the factors which are correlated while holding the Control key down
  3. Click on button Group
  4. Select the newly created factor group and click on the 'Edit...' button to specify its multivariate distribution, factor means and covariance matrix
  5. Click on OK to close the factor group editor.
