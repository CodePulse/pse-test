Furnace

The Furnace model is used to simulate the cracking of hydrocarbons in the radiant section of a steam cracking furnace to produce olefins. Both molecular and radical based kinetic mechanisms are available for cracking of gas feedstocks (e.g. ethane, propane). A radical based kinetic mechanism is available for simulating naphtha cracking. In addition, the effect of coking is also taken into account. It is possible to use custom cracking and coking kinetics if necessary.

Several predefined standard radiant coil configurations are available (see 'Standard coil geometries'). In addition to this, it is possible to customise the Furnace model to use a custom coil if the desired coil configuration is not available.
