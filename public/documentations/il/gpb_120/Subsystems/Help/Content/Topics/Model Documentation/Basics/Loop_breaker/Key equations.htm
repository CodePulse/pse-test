Key equations

The relations used in the loop breaker depend on which mode is chosen and whether the loop breaker is open or closed. During flowsheet initialisation the loop breaker is switched from open to closed. Note that depending on which mode is chosen, stream conditions specified in the loop breaker model are either specifications or initial guesses.

Flow-driven mode

In flow-driven mode, the outputs of the loop breaker model are equal to the specifications made in the dialog of the loop breaker. When the loop breaker is open, the relations are as follows (N.B. that the specification for enthalpy can be in the form of temperature, vapour fraction etc.):

When the loop breaker is closed in flow-driven mode, the outlet enthalpy becomes equal to the inlet enthalpy, which closes the energy balance in the loop. Note that the flowrate, pressure and composition remain equal to the specified values. For the flowrate and the compostion, the fact that no material enters or leaves the loop and that there are no chemical reactions occurring in the loop, ensures that the inlet composition and flowrate is equal to the outlet composition and flowrate.
