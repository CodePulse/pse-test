Valve characteristic

The lift of the valve occurring in the liquid and vapour flow-pressure drop equations varies, between 0 and 1, as a function of the inlet pressure (and other parameters) and depends on the type of pressure relief valve. This function is known as the characteristic curve. For conventional spring-operated or balanced-bellows valves the following functions are available: spring or balanced bellows curve, linear, equal percentage, and quick opening. For pilot-operated valves the following functions are available: modulated action and pop action. In addition to this, it is possible to specify a curve hysteresis and thus the valve behaviour when opening and closing will be different.

Pilot-operated with pop action

A pilot-operated valve with a pop action will open and close instantly, so it will only be fully open or completely closed. If the curve has no hysteresis, then the valve will open or close at the set pressure. If the curve has hysteresis, then switching from closed to open occurs if the inlet pressure is higher than the set pressure and switching from open to closed occurs if the inlet pressure is lower than the closing pressure.
