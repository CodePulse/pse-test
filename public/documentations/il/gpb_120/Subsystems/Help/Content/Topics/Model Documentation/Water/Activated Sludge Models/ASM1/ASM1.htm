ASM1

The Activated Sludge Model N°1 essentially predicts carbon oxidation, nitrification, and de-nitrification phenomena by heterotroph and autotroph groups of bacteria, present in activated sludge basins. All model details can be found in the International Water Association scientific and technical report n°9. The gML Water implementation is similar to the original published model, with the following three differences:

  • The component Snd is expressed as gCOD/m3 rather than gN/m3. An equivalent quantity of nitrogen can be easily deduced from the component atomic composition and its nitrogen content.
  • The component Xnd is expressed as gCOD/m3 rather than gN/m3. An equivalent quantity of nitrogen can be easily deduced from the component atomic composition and its nitrogen content.
  • An additional group of bacteria is considered in the default model : the methylotrophs group. Including this group of bacteria allows prediction of methanol consumption and associated phenomena in the default ASM1 model. This group of bacteria is modelled using the inherent structure of ASM1 model for describing growth and decay processes.

Biomass decay

This process describes decay of bacteria in the water. This complex phenomena is modelled using first order kinetics. A death-regeneration concept is considered in the ASM1 model structure so the decay produces slowly biodegradable substrates plus some inert matter that cannot be further degraded.

Atomic Balance equations

Four balance equations for each reaction ensure that Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD) and atomic balances are closed (COD, N, C, P). Note that O will normally be balanced using the water component. However, due to the nature of the water streams considered (composed of more than 99.9 % of water), it is not necessary to include this balance systematically in the models. The electro neutrality equation used to determine the pH will implicitly close the H balance (see 'Electrolyte system equilibriums').

The components used for this purpose are the oxygen, the total inorganic nitrogen, the total inorganic carbon and total inorganic phosphorous.
