D02b - Styrene reactor - Radial inward flow

In this example, the styrene production process is modelled considering a radial flow reactor with 1D distributed pellet model and the process stream flowing inward through the catalyst bed.

Two different reactor configurations are considered for this application: one where the inlet stream enters the catalyst bed from both the top and the outer side; and another one where the process stream enters the catalyst bed from only the outer side. For this last configuration, the two reactor models are configured to either neglect the pressure drop in the inlet shaft (uniform inlet mode) or to explicitly account for it.

The processes (D02b_Axial_Radial_in_Flow_Styrene_Reactor_Initialisation, D02b_Radial_in_Flow_Styrene_Reactor_Initialisation and D02b_Radial_in_Flow_Styrene_Reactor_Uniform_inlet_Initialisation) can be used to initialise the models from poor initial guesses - the existing Saved Variable Sets will be updated if the option to auto-update is selected upon starting a simulation. The remainder processes use the saved variable sets to initialise the simulation.
