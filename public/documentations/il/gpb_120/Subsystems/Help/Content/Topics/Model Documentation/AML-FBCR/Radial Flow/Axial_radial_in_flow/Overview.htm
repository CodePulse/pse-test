Catalyst pellets section axial radial in flow

This model represents an annular fixed-bed reactor filled with solid pellets. Gas flows in radially from the outer surface and axially from the top of the fixed-bed. The process gas flows out of the fixed-bed at the centre of the reactor.

The fixed-bed model is axially and radially distributed (2D and considers separate conservation equations for fluid and catalyst (heterogeneous bed model). The pellets are represented either by a lumped model or by a distributed model considering the multi-component mass transfer inside catalyst pores.

The flow distribution over the inlet shaft can be considered. This takes into account the pressure distribution in this inlet channel. Alternatively, a uniform inlet flow distribution can be used.

Internal cooling can be enabled. In this case heat will be exchanged with internal cooling segments represented by a simplified two-dimensional (axial and radial) coolant model. This coolant model must be connected using the FBRDistributedThermalContact_2D port.

For more information on the empirical correlations, see 'Correlations'.
