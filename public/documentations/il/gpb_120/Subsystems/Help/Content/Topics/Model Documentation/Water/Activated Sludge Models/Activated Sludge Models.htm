Activated Sludge Models (ASMs)

Activated Sludge Models (ASMs) is a generic name given to models that are used to determine, from a macroscopic point of view, the biochemical reactions taking place in Activated Sludge systems.

These models were first published by a group of researchers members of the International Water Association (IWA). For a detailed review of the model features and limitations, please refer to IWA technical reports.

The predictions of these models are the net production or consumptions rates of biological compounds that are a result of the biological processes in the activated sludge. Energy dissipation by the biological processes is also taken into account by these models. The ASM models are used in all process unit models in the gML Water library that involve biological treatment.

The gML Water library includes the ASM1, ASM2d and the ASM3 model. This section describes the general architecture of the ASM models implemented within the gML Water library, using the ASM1 model as an example.
