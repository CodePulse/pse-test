Dynamics

The Dynamics tab is used to insert dynamic mode and initial conditions data.

Name Default Unit Description
Dynamics Selector
Off
Simulates the unit in steady-state mode. If this option is selected, the unit will always be in steady-state mode.
On
Simulates the unit in dynamic mode. If this option is selected, changes in time for mass, momentum and energy holdups will be automatically calculated.

Dynamic momentum balance

 Selector
Off
Turns off dynamics in the momentum balance.
On
Turns on dynamics in the momentum balance.

Initial conditions specifications

 Selector
Steady-state
The model is initially at steady-state.
Dynamic
The initial values for all the state variables of the unit must be specified.

Initial holdup

Selector
Composition (w), pressure (P) and intensive variable (P, T, h)
Determines the initial material holdup in the vessel from the composition and an intensive variable (pressure, temperature or enthalpy).
Overall holdup (M_T), pressure (P) and composition (w)
Determines the initial material holdup in the vessel from the overall holdup and the composition.

Holdup specification

 Selector
Mass
Selects mass basis for the specification of holdup or composition.
Molar
Selects molar basis for the specification of holdup or composition.

Initial thermal specification Selector

Vapour fraction
Enthalpy
Saturated liquid (Vapour fraction = 0)
When selecting this option, the vesseloutlet stream will initially consist of a saturated liquid (i.e. the vesseloutlet stream temperature is the bubble temperature of the mixture).
Saturated vapour (Vapour fraction = 1)
When selecting this option, the vesseloutlet stream will initially consist of a saturated vapour (i.e. the vesseloutlet stream temperature is the dew temperature of the mixture).
Subcooled liquid
When selecting this option, the vesseloutlet stream will initially consist of a subcooled liquid (i.e. outlet temperature is calculated using the provided degree of subcooling relative to the saturated liquid).
Superheated vapour
When selecting this option, the vesseloutlet stream will initially consist of a superheated vapour (i.e. outlet temperature is calculated using the provided degree of superheating relative to the saturated vapour).
