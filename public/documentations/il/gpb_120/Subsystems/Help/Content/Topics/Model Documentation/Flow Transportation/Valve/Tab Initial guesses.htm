Initial guesses

The Initial guesses tab is used to provide initial guesses for some variables in order to improve the speed and robustness of initialisation.

Name Default Unit Description
Outlet specification Selector
Pressure
Outlet stream pressure.
Pressure drop
Pressure drop specification. The pressure drop is the difference between inlet and outlet pressure of the valve.
Flow coefficient
Valve flow coefficient.
Temperature
Vapour fraction
Saturated liquid (Vapour fraction = 0)
When selecting this option, the outlet stream will initially consist of a saturated liquid (i.e. the outlet stream temperature is the bubble temperature of the mixture).
Saturated vapour (Vapour fraction = 1)
When selecting this option, the outlet stream will initially consist of a saturated vapour (i.e. the outlet stream temperature is the dew temperature of the mixture).
Subcooled liquid
When selecting this option, the outlet stream will initially consist of a subcooled liquid (i.e. outlet temperature is calculated using the provided degree of subcooling relative to the saturated liquid).
Superheated vapour
When selecting this option, the outlet stream will initially consist of a superheated vapour (i.e. outlet temperature is calculated using the provided degree of superheating relative to the saturated vapour).
Flowrate specification Selector
Mass
Mass basis is selected for flowrate specification.
Molar
Molar basis is selected for flowrate specification.
Pressure bar Initial guess for the outlet stream pressure.
Pressure drop kPa Initial guess for the pressure drop.
Flow coefficient

kg s-1 Pa-1

 Initial guess for the valve flow coefficient.
Temperature K Initial guess for the outlet stream temperature.
Vapour fraction kg/kg Initial guess for the outlet stream vapour fraction.
Degree of subcooling K

Initial guess for the difference between the saturated liquid temperature (bubble temperature) and the actual temperature of the outlet stream.
Degree of superheating K

Initial guess for the difference between the actual temperature of the outlet streamand the saturated vapour temperature (dew temperature).
