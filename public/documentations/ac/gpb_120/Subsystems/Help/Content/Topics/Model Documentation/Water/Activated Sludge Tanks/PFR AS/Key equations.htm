Key equations

The Activated Sludge Models (ASMs) used to compute the biochemical reactions are described in the section 'Activated Sludge Models (ASMs)' The documentation for the model used to compute the pH and the electrolytes system can be found at 'Electrolyte system equilibriums'

The components involved in the mass balances are specific to the activated sludge and water chemistry model in use.

This model assume a constant water volume and a constant pressure, but takes into account the hydrostatic pressure to determine the gas-liquid mass transfer.

Mass reaction rate

The mass reaction rate for each component j is the difference between the net component production rate by biochemical reactions in the ASM submodel and the liquid-gas transfer rate.

All mass transfer rates are computed at each point of a longitudinal grid.
