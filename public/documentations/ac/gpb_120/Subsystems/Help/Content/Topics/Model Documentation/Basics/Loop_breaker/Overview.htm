Loop breaker

The Loop breaker model is included in a flowsheet to determine the flowrate, composition and the pressures of closed material loops. Examples of this include refrigeration loops and steam cycles.

In flow-driven mode the pressure, flowrate and composition in the loop are specified, whereas in pressure-driven mode either the pressure or flowrate is specified and the other quantity (pressure or flowrate) is calculated from the flow-pressure relationships defined by the unit operation models in the loop.

The model is also used to aid initialisation by providing initial guesses (e.g. temperature or specific enthalpy).

The use of a Loop breaker is required if the following conditions are met:

  1. No material enters or leaves the loop and there are no chemical reactions occurring in unit operations in the loop(i.e. it is a completely closed loop).
  2. For all units in the loop, the simulation mode is either steady-state or dynamic with steady-state initial conditions.

If these conditions are not met a Recycle breaker ('Recycle breaker') should be used instead.
