Distillation column

This model describes a two-phase (vapour-liquid) distillation column. The column is divided into multiple stages. For each stage a mass and energy balance are determined and by default it is assumed that vapour-liquid equilibrium is achieved at each stage.

In the condenser and reboiler it is also assumed that vapour-liquid equilibrium is achieved. Columns with total or partial condensers and kettle or thermosyphon type reboilers can be modelled. The column model can also represent columns with liquid and vapour side-draws.

Depending on the type of condenser the model requires either two or three operational specifications. For a column with a total condenser the two specifications determine the energy rate supplied to the reboiler and the ratio between the reflux (R) and distillate (D) flowrate. For a column with a partial condenser an additional specification can be made that determines the cooling rate in the condenser.

Non-ideal trays can be modelled using the Murphree efficiency.
