Recycle breaker

The Recycle breaker is required to initialise flowsheets with recycle(s) of material. A recycle stream is a stream that is mixed back at a point upstream in the process. For each stream which is recycled, a recycle breaker needs to be inserted into the stream at a point before that it is mixed back.

The recycle breaker allows the specification of an initial guess for some or all of the stream properties. When converging flowsheets with recycles, this initial guess is used to determine an initial solution for the flowsheet, with the recycle breaker open (i.e. the inlet is not connected to the outlet). Then, this initial solution is used as an initial guess for the solution of the whole flowsheet with the recycle breaker closed (i.e. the inlet is connected to the outlet and the specified initial guesses are no longer used).

Determining what the initial guess for a particular recycle stream may require insight into the process being modelled. Specifying an initial guess that is close to the stream conditions of the solution of the flowsheet may aid in convergence, while specifying an initial guess that is very different from the stream conditions of the solution of the flowsheet might prevent convergence to the solution. In many cases, when a recycle breaker is placed at the point just before the recycle stream is mixed back into the main stream, an initial guess with a zero mass flowrate may be sufficient. When the initial guess for the mass flowrate is zero, the other stream properties (composition, temperature) do not require an initial guess.
