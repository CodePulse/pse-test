Equilibrium flash methods in Multiflash

The interface to Multiflash supports all the methods listed in the topic in 'The set of physical properties supported by gPROMS', including the flash methods, which are listed below for convenience. This topic is concerned with the organisation of result vectors of the methods.

Equilibrium flash calculations
Property Name Inputs Description Type
PHFlash P,H,n Equilibrium pressure and enthalpy flash Vector
PHTemperature P,H,n Equilibrium temperature of a pressure and enthalpy flash Scalar
PHVolume P,H,n Equilibrium volume of a pressure and enthalpy flash Scalar
PSFlash P,S,n Equilibrium pressure and entropy flash Vector
PVFlash P,V,n Equilibrium pressure and volume flash Vector
PVFMassFlash P,Vapour mass fraction,n Equilibrium flash given pressure and vapour mass fraction Vector
PVFMolFlash P,Vapour molar fraction,n Equilibrium flash given pressure and vapour molar fraction Vector
PVFVolFlash P,Vapour volume fraction,n Equilibrium flash given pressure and vapour volume fraction Vector
TPFlash T,P,n Equilibrium temperature and pressure flash Vector
TVFlash T,P,n Equilibrium temperature and volume flash Vector
TVFMassFlash T,Vapour mass fraction,n Equilibrium flash given temperature and vapour mass fraction Vector
TVFMolFlash T,Vapour molar fraction,n Equilibrium flash given temperature and vapour molar fraction Vector
TVFVolFlash T,Vapour volume fraction,n Equilibrium flash given temperature and vapour volume fraction Vector
UVFlash U,V,n Equilibrium internal energy and volume flash Vector

The results vector for the methods listed in the table above is of length 11 + 3 * NoComp, except for PHFlashExtended which has a length of 22 + 3 * NoComp. The information is organised as detailed in the table below.

An important point to note is that up to two liquid phases are assumed to be present at equilibrium. Consequently, the flash methods and results can handle a VLLE system. If a particular phase does not exist under the specified conditions, then its amount and all of its intensive properties are returned as zero.
