What is Global System Analysis?

Global System Analysis (GSA) permits the investigation of the global behaviour of a system. In contrast to simulation, in which specific values are assigned to system inputs and the values of key outputs are reported, in GSA it is possible to specify a range of input values of interest and therefore obtain a range of outputs (see figure below).

Inputs are known as factors, and outputs as responses. Typical factors are:

  • Decisions made with respect to:
    • Design (for example, reactor length)
    • Operation (for example, operating temperature).
  • Environmental factors, such as:
    • External disturbances (for example, feed composition)
    • Commercial environment (for example, market prices or demand)
    • Model parameters, among which you want to identify the most influential.

In contrast, responses are generally key performance indicators (KPIs) such as product purity, safety constraints and cost of operation or design.
