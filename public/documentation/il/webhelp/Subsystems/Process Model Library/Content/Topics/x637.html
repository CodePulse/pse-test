Units of measurement

Standard International (SI) units are used throughout the PML models. So, for example, the units of pressure are Pascal (Pa) and the units of mass are kilogram (kg). Mass units are used in the models (i.e. kg, kg/s and mass fractions). Where necessary the mass units are converted to local molar values (e.g. for reactions and/or phase equilibria).

When industrial standards for certain model equations are not SI, then the variables or parameters that characterise the model will use the industrially-accepted standard. Conversions to SI takes place within the model. For example, control valves are sized in Cv units based upon US gallons per minute and psi. However, note that the valve model uses SI units internally (i.e. Pa rather than psi and kg/s rather than gallons/minute). Refer to each model's own documentation for further details.
