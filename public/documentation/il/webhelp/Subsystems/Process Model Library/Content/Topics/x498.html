Model Class (Node or Connector)

The PML is based upon a Node and Connector structure, with all equipment models being strongly typed as either a Node or a Connector. Non-equipment models, such as those relating to process control are not classified as Nodes or Connectors. Composite models, such as a column section are classified as Hybrid, since they have both Node and Connector characteristics.

Nodes calculate properties such as pressure, enthalpy and composition from the accumulation of mass and energy. Typical examples of Nodes include vessels or tanks. NOTE:Nodes may only be linked together via a Connector model.

Connectors calculate the flow rate and direction of flow between Nodes (based on the values of inlet and outlet information variables). Connectors may be pressure loss devices, such as pipes and valves, as well as pressure gain devices, such as pumps and compressors. Connectors can be linked to both Node and Connector models.

The model library has been written so that both irreversible and reversible flow may be accommodated. All reversibility is handled in the Connector models based on the pressures in the adjacent models. Having decided on the flow direction, the Connector model inherits the intensive properties (composition and enthalpy) of the adjacent model unit with the highest pressure.
