Assumptions

The following assumptions are made in deriving the model of the stirred tank reactor:

  • The reactor is perfectly mixed at all times (that is, there are no spatial variations in intensive properties).
  • Phase thermal equilibria is assumed
  • Only liquid-phase irreversible chemical reactions take place. No nuclear reactions take place.
  • Partition coefficients are temperature independent.
  • Mass transfer coefficients are independent from temperature and flow conditions.
  • The pressure is constant within the reactor.
  • The reactor contents are incompressible.
  • No particle transformation events (for example, aggregation or breakage) take place.
