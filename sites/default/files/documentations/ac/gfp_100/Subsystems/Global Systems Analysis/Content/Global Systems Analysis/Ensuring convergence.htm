Ensuring convergence

Typically, the optimal number of samples is unknown beforehand, that is, the minimum number of samples which produces accurate results. The optimal number of samples is case specific and depends on the attributes below, among others:

  • System non-linearity, that is, the degree of non-linear equations and, particularly, relations between factors and responses
  • System complexity, for example, the number of recycles, components and heat integration, among others
  • Which factors and responses are selected as well as their number
  • Analysis type and method (uncertainty, elementary effects or variance-based sensitivity).

Experience will help with the accurate estimation of the correct number of sample points. To check for convergence, it is recommended that the analysis is executed for different numbers of samples. By plotting the key metric of interest, for example, the total effect index, against the number of samples, it is possible to verify whether sufficient sample points have been generated.
