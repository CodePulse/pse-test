Key equations

In the double-pipe heat exchanger a one-dimensionally distributed mass, energy and momentum balance is used for each fluid. The two fluids present are the inner pipe fluid and the outer pipe fluid, and they can flow in either a co-current or counter-current arrangement.

Inner pipe fluid mass and energy balances

In case there is more than one unit in parallel, it is assumed that the flow is perfectly distributed over the units. This means that all parallel units can be represented by mass and energy balances for a single unit.
