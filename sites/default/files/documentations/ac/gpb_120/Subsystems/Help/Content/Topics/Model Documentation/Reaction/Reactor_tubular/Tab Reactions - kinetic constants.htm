Reactions - kinetic constants

The Reactions - kinetic constants tab is used to specify the parameters related to the kinetic constants of the reactions taking place.

Name Default Unit Description
Kinetic constants calculation Selector
Arrhenius - pre-exponential factors (k' = k0*exp(-Ea/RT))
Calculates the rates of reaction from the Arrhenius law.
Arrhenius - kinetic constants at the reference temperature (k' = kref*exp(-Ea/R*(1/T - 1/Tref)))
Calculates the rates of reaction from the Arrhenius law and a reference temperature.
Temperature correction Selector
Off (k=k')
No temperature correction is used.
On (k=k'*(T^n))
Uses a temperature correction for the pre-exponential factor.
Forward rate pre-exponential factors (k0) Units depend on the composition basis

Pre-exponential factor used in the Arrhenius law. The units of this constant depend on both the concentration basis choice and the reaction order. With m the order of the reaction the units for the pre-exponential factor are:

  • Molar concentrations:  h-1(kmol-1 m3)(m-1)
  • Mass fractions: kmol h-1m-3
  • Molar fractions: kmol h-1m-3
  • Mass concentrations: kmol h-1m-3(kg-1 m3)(m-1)
  • Activities: h-1(kmol-1 m3)(m-1)
  • Fugacities: kmol h-1 m-3bar-m
  • Partial pressures: kmol h-1 m-3bar-m
Kinetic constant (kref) Units depend on the composition basis Kinetic constant used in the Arrhenius law with reference temperature.
Forward rate activation energy (Ea) J/mol Activation energy used in the Arrhenius law.
Reference temperature (Tref) K Reference temperature for the Arrhenius law.
Temperature power (n) - Exponent used for the temperature correction.
