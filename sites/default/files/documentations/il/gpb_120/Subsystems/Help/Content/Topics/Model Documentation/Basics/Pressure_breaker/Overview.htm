Pressure breaker

The Pressure breaker model can be used to aid in initialising dynamic, pressure-driven flowsheets. In these types of flowsheets, flowrates are determined by upstream and downstream pressures and, as a consequence, the entire flowsheet becomes coupled. Since a downstream pressure affects upstream flowrates, this can pose problems when initialising these flowsheets in steady-state conditions. Inserting a Pressure breaker into the flowsheet will decouple the part of the flowsheet that is downstream from the Pressure breaker from the part that is upstream, during initialisation.

The decoupling of part of the flowsheet that is downstream from the Pressure breaker is achieved by providing an initial guess for either the pressure (inlet or outlet) or the flowrate (inlet or outlet) that is used during initialisation. During initialisation, the outlet pressure is not set to be equal to the inlet pressure.
