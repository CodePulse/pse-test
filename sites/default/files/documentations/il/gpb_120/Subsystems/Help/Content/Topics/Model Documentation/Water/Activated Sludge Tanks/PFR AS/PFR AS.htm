PFR AS - Plug-Flow Reactor (Activated Sludge)

The "Plug-Flow Reactor" or PFR model uses a biological and a water chemistry model to compute the biochemical reactions that take place in a Plug-Flow Reactor (PFR) Activated Sludge tank. This reactor is modelled as being distributed in the direction of the flow. The reactor is aerated, and the oxygen transfer rate coefficient (Kla) can be computed according to the model selected in the specifications. The number and the position of sensors to measure oxygen, ammonia and others quantities along the axis can also be modified via the specifications. This process unit operation can be used with any available biological and water chemistry model.

The biochemical reactions as well as the gas - liquid mass transfer are automatically set-up from the selected biological and water chemistry models.

The gas transfer performance depends strongly on the type of aeration, which can be selected in the model specifications dialog. This model also support non-homogeneous air distribution along the longitudinal axis. The Heat transfer in the reactor can also be considered.
