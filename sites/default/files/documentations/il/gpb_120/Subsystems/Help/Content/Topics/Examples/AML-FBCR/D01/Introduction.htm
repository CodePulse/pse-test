D01 - Acrylic acid reactor

Acrylic acid is primarily used as an intermediate product in the production of acrylates. Acrylic esters are used to produce polymers (polyacrylates) which are used for coatings, paints, adhesives and binders for leather, paper and textiles1Acrylic Acid and Derivatives, Ullmann's Encyclopedia of Industrial Chemistry, 2012, (c) Wiley-VCH..

Acrylic acid is mainly produced via heterogeneous catalytic oxidation of propylene with air and steam. The commercial process involves two catalytic reaction steps: the first is oxidation of propylene to acrolein, and the second is oxidation of acrolein to propylene using different catalysts. Both reaction steps involve fixed-bed reactors but this example focuses on the second step and how the AML:FBCR can be used to model different reactor configurations.
