Pressure drop through the aerated liquid correlations

Three distinct correlations are implemented for the estimation of the pressure drop through the aerated liquid hl: the correlation from Bennett (Perry, 1999, p. 14-32, 14-33)4Perry, R.H. & Green, D.W., 1999. Chemical Engineers’ Handbook, 7th Ed., McGraw-Hill., the correlation from Fair (Perry, 1999, p. 14-31)5Perry, R.H. & Green, D.W., 1999. Chemical Engineers’ Handbook, 7th Ed., McGraw-Hill. and the hydrostatic equation.

The correlations used to predict pressure drop through the aerated liquid rely on having a correlation that predicts the effective clear liquid height (for these see 'Tray holdup correlations'). Depending on the source or author, it may contain a correlation for either the clear liquid height only, the pressure drop through the aerated liquid only or both. Although correlations can be selected separately for the clear liquid height and the pressure drop through the aerated liquid, it might be advisable to select two corresponding ones from the same source author or source if possible (e.g. the correlations from Bennett can be selected for both the clear liquid height and the pressure drop through the aerated liquid).
