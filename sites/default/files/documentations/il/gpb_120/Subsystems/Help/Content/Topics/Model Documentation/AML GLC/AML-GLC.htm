AML:GLC

The gPROMS® Advanced Model Library for Gas-Liquid Contactors (AML:GLC) can be used to accurately predict the performance of separation stages where the degree of separation is restricted by mass and heat transfer limitations or reaction kinetics. When this is the case, models that assume phase or reaction equilibrium will over predict the degree of separation that can be achieved. The models contained in the AML:GLC can be used to accurately determine the rates of mass and heat transfer as well as kinetics and thereby provide accurate predictive modelling for these separation systems.

The library includes the following key features:

  • Steady-state modelling of packed columns where the degree of separation is limited by mass and heat transfer or reaction kinetics
  • Assumption of stagnant films between liquid and vapour phase in which diffusion is the rate-limiting process for mass and energy transport between the liquid and vapour phase
  • Rigorous modelling of the mass and heat transport and the reaction kinetics in the stagnant films
  • Prediction of the interfacial area between the liquid and vapour phase based on hydrodynamics in the column
  • Prediction of the thickness of the stagnant film in the liquid and vapour phase based on hydrodynamics in the column.

The models have integrated support for a variety of rigorous physical property packages such as PSE's gSAFT, KBC’s Multiflash and other CAPE-OPEN compliant packages.

This document describes the library and its application, including specification instructions for each model.
