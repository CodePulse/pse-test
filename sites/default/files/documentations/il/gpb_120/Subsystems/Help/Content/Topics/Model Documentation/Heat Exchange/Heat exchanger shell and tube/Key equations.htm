Key equations

In the shell and tube heat exchanger a one-dimensionally distributed mass, energy and momentum balance is used for each fluid.

Tube-side fluid mass and energy balances

The shell and tube heat exchanger can contain one or more tubes. In case there are more tubes, it is assumed that the flow is perfectly distributed over the tubes and that the there is no radial temperature profile. This means that all tubes can be represented by a single tube mass and energy balance. Shell-side fluid

Geometry

The Bell-Delaware model can be used to model a shell containing a tube bundle and baffles. This model takes into account the different bypass streams that can exist in a baffled shell and for a given shell and tube bundle geometry calculates the corresponding heat transfer coefficient and pressure drop.
