Gas cooled bed pellets section

This model represents a section of a multibular reactor with solid pellets. The catalyst is on the shell side and the cooling gas on the tube side. The shell-side process fluid can be either gas or liquid that will flow through the fixed-bed.

The fixed-bed model is axially distributed (1D) and considers separate conservation equations for fluid and catalyst (heterogeneous bed model). The pellets are represented either by a lumped model or by a distributed model considering the multi-component mass transfer inside catalyst pores.

The cooling gas enters and leaves the system in co-current or in counter-current configuration with respect to the reactor feed.

Heat is exchanged with the distributed internal cooling tubes through a distributed wall (1D wall). There is no heat transfer from the shell to the outside.

For more information on the empirical correlations, see 'Correlations'.
