Adsorption bed

The Adsorption bed model describes an adsorption vessel. The vessel contains a packed bed with adsorbent particles. Gas flows through the bed and species can be adsorbed onto the surface of the adsorbent material.

In this model the temperatures, pressures and species concentrations are considered to be distributed in one dimension (1-D distributed model). Depending on whether axial or radial flow is selected, this distribution is either in the axial or radial direction. The model describes the mass, energy and momentum transport of the gas flowing through the bed, as well as the mass and energy transport from the gas phase to the surface of the adsorbent material. Both convective and dispersive transport are modelled. To predict adsorption onto the surface of the adsorbent, a Linear Driving Force (LDF) boundary-layer model is used. The ability of different species to adsorb under certain conditions is described by isotherm models. These predict the amount of each species that is adsorbed when the gas phase and the adsorbed phase are at equilibrium.

The bed can either be assumed to be operated isothermally, adiabatically or the heat transfer between the fluid phase and the bed wall and between the bed wall and the environment can be taken into account. The thermal dynamics of the bed wall can be also be predicted.

This model is dynamic and can handle flow-reversal. It can be used to model Pressure-Swing Adsorption (PSA) and Temperature-Swing Adsorption (TSA) process models.

Using the custom modelling functionality in gPROMS ProcessBuilder, parts of this model can be customized to cater for functionality that is not covered by the standard model. These parts include:

  • Isotherms
  • Mass transfer correlations for the mass transfer from the fluid phase to the adsorbed phase.
  • Correlations to predict axial dispersion in the bed
  • Correlations to predict fluid phase physical properties
