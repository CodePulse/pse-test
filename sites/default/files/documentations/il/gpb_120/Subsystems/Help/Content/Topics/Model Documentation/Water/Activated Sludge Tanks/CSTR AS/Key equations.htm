Key equations

The Activated Sludge Models (ASMs) used to compute the biochemical reactions are described in the section 'Activated Sludge Models (ASMs)', and the electrolyte system model used to compute the pH is described in the section 'Electrolyte system equilibriums'

The components involved in the mass balances depend on the type of activated sludge and water chemistry model used.

This model assumes a constant water volume and a constant pressure, but takes into account the hydrostatic pressure to determine gas-liquid mass transfer.

Mass reaction rate

The Mass reaction rate for each component j is the difference between the net component production rate by biochemical reactions, the electrolytes system equilibrium and the liquid-gas transfer rate. Note that the electrolytes system equilibrium influences the pH and consequently impacts the biology kinetics as well as the liquid-gas mass transfer.
