D04 - Methanol reactor

Methanol is one of the key raw materials in the chemical industry. It is mainly used as an intermediate product for the synthesis of formaldehyde, methyl tert-butyl ether (MTBE), acetic acid, dimethyl ether (DME), propylene and others, and as an energy source or solvent.

Current industrial methanol production is based on the low-pressure catalytic conversion of synthesis gas. Various reactor designs and operation modes are used in practice, including axial, radial and axial-radial flow reactors; in adiabatic or quasi-isothermal operation, with cooling medium in the shell or in the tubes (the latter are also referred to as gas cooled reactors). The AML:FBCR can be used to model this process and this example illustrates its application to adiabatic radial flow reactors.
