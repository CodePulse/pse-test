Coordinate transformation – ellipsoid

The ellipsoid is expressed by a well-known correlation. To implement a coordinate transformation which returns an ellipsoid, consider the ellipsoid in the figure below:

Example of an ellipsoid

This ellipsoid can be described in terms of radius r and angle α like so:

Notation

  a Parameter representing half the width of the ellipsoid
  b Parameter representing half the length of the ellipsoid
  r Radius of ellipsoid
  xh Variable or factor
  xc Centre point of the ellipsoid for variable/factor x
  yh Variable or factor
  yc Centre point of the ellipsoid for variable/factor y
  a Angle
  q Angle between the ellipsoid and X-axis

Note that this transformation changes the distribution in the space. For example, consider a flash separation of a 50-50 mass% mixture of hexane and heptane at a flow rate of 2 kg/s and specify the sum of the amount of hexane in the gas product and the amount of heptane in the liquid product as the objective function.
