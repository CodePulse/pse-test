Assumptions

The following assumptions are made in deriving the model of the plug flow reactor:

  • Inlet streams are perfectly mixed when entering the unit.
  • No energy transfer to the environment.
  • Phase thermal equilibria is assumed
  • Transport of the material is governed by a plug flow description with dispersion in the axial direction.
  • No dispersion in the radial direction.
  • Only liquid-phase irreversible chemical reactions take place. No nuclear reactions take place.
  • The pressure is constant within the reactor.
  • The reactor contents are incompressible.
  • No particle transformation events (for example, aggregation or breakage) take place.
