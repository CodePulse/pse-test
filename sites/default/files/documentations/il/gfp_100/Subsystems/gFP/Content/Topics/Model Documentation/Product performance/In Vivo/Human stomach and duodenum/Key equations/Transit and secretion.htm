It is reporting that approximately nine litres of fluid pass through the gastrointestinal system each day. On average two litres are ingested, the rest represents secretions. Very little is excreted, so nearly all of the fluid is reabsorbed. Secretions come from various sources; gastric and saliva secretions in the stomach, mucous, bile, Brunner’s glands, and pancreatic secretions in the small intestine, as tabulated below:

Secretions in the in vivo model are comprised of constant rate secretions which have been calculated based on the aggregate of secretion rates reported in the literature, and first order dynamic secretions, which are calculated as a function of the fluid volume flowing into the intestinal compartment. Secretions are considered to consist of water only and thus do not account for pH and composition (these are accounted for via other parts of the framework).
