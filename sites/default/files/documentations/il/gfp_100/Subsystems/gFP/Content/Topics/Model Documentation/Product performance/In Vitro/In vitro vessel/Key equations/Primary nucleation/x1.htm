A further distinction can be made between homogeneous and heterogeneous primary nucleation, corresponding to situations without and with the presence of particulate matter (e.g. dust particles) that can facilitate the primary nucleation process. In practice, some particulate matter is always present, i.e. homogeneous primary nucleation is rarely observed.  From the modelling perspective, the only difference is the order of magnitude of the pre-exponential factor in the rate expression.

The classical primary nucleation rate expression contains two parameters that need to be estimated from experimental data, viz the pre-exponential factor and the surface energy correction factor. As the pre-exponential factor can be as high as 1030, the expression is rewritten as shown below.
