Chemical reaction equilibria are considered for acidic/basic drugs, drug binding to bile micelles and physiological buffers. When specifying dissociation chemical equilibria, the convention used is that the first dissociation always involves the loss of a proton from the most protonated form of the molecule.

The default reactions for the in vivo models includes the dissociation of the carbonic acid into the bicarbonate ion, the dissociation of the bicarbonate ion into the carbonate ion and the self-ionisation of water. Additional reactions are added based on the compound type (neutral, acid, di-acid, base, di-base, and ordinary ampholyte).
