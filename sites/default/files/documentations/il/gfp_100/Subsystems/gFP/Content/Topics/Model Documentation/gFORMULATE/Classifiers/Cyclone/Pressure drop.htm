Pressure drop

The determination of the pressure drop in the cyclone follows the model of Chen J et al. (2007) 1Chen J, Shi M. A universal model to calculate cyclone pressure drop. Powder Tech., 171(2007), 184-191.

Note: In the equations below, parameters to be specified or estimated are printed in blue.

The pressure drop in the cyclone is assumed to be the sum of a local pressure drop and a frictional pressure drop. The local pressure drop consists of an expansion loss at the cyclone inlet and a contraction loss at the cyclone outlet. The frictional loss consists of a swirling loss due to friction between the gas flow and the cyclone wall and a dissipation loss of the gas dynamic energy in the outlet.
