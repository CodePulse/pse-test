Collision frequency and impact energy

The probabilities and impact velocities of crystal-impeller collisions are calculated using a geometrically simplified model developed by Ploß et al.1Ploβ, R., T. Tengler, A. Mersmann (1989). A new model of the effect of stirring intensity on the rate of secondary nucleation. Chem. Eng. Technol., 12, 137-146.. This model provides an approximation for the complex two-phase flow pattern in the vicinity of the impeller. Both the probabilities and impact velocities are a function of the distance from the impeller axis. Therefore, in addition to the discretisation of the crystal size co-ordinate, the impeller blade needs to be discretised along its radius. The probability and impact velocity calculations are performed independently for the faces and edges of the impeller blades.
