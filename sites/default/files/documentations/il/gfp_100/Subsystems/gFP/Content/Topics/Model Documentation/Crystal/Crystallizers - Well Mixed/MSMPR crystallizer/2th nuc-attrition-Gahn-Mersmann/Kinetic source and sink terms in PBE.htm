Kinetic source and sink terms in PBE

 

Relationship between the attrition related sink and source terms in the population balance equation (after Mitrovic1 Mitrovic, A. (2002). Population Balance based Modeling, Simulation, Analysis and Control of Crystallization Processes, PhD thesis, University of Stuttgart, Germany.)

The net source term as a result of attrition, f±n,attr, is composed of three terms, as shown in , which relate to the various steps of the attrition process: a sink term for the parent crystals that are subject to attrition, f-n,attr, a source term reflecting the birth of a single crystal, slightly smaller than the parent crystal, f+n,attr,1, and a source term for the attrition fragments, f+n,attr,2.  

(1)

The collision rate of crystals with size, L, at radial position, r, is given by [Eq. 1] in 'Collision frequency and impact energy'. However, not all these collisions will necessarily result in attrition of the parent crystal. The other condition that must be satisfied to initiate an attrition event is a collision energy exceeding the minimum collision energy as given by [Eq. 3] in 'Removed attrition volume'.
