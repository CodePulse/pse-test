Consolidation

Consolidation is characterised by a reduction in particle size due to a decrease in internal porosity. This model considers the internal pore space to be the sum of the intra-granule void and the internal liquid. Consolidation is treated as a negative growth term in the population balance model, resulting from a decrease in liquid and internal pore space. The mass of all other components in the granulate phase is conserved. The loss of liquid from the granulate phase is balanced with an increase in external liquid.
