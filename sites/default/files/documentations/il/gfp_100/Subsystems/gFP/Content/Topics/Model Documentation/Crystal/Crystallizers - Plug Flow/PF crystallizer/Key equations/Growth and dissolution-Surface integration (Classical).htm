Surface integration (Classical)

Surface integration involves surface diffusion of the solute molecule/ion to a growth site, orientation (rotation) of the solute molecule/ion in order to slot into the crystal lattice, and the actual integration into the lattice.

When crystals dissolve, there is no need for surface diffusion and orientation.  The solute molecule/ion simply leaves the crystal lattice and starts diffusing through the liquid boundary layer surrounding the crystal.  In this case, rate limitation is solely in the mass transfer step, which is reflected by replacing the equation above with the equation below for undersaturated conditions.
